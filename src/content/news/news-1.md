## Overview

Today, we are releasing version 2.0 of our Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP), a comprehensive framework that governs how AXEROAI approaches the development and deployment of increasingly capable AI systems.

The RSP is one of the most important commitments we make — both to ourselves and to the public. It sets concrete, measurable thresholds that determine what safety mitigations must be in place before we train or deploy a new generation of models.

## What's New in v2.0

Version 2.0 builds substantially on our original RSP with several key additions:

### Clearer Capability Thresholds

We've introduced more precise definitions of what we call "AI Safety Levels" (ASL), based on measurable capability benchmarks across domains including:

- **Autonomous replication**: Can the model take actions to acquire resources, evade shutdown, or copy itself?
- **Uplift to CBRN risks**: Does the model provide meaningful assistance in creating biological, chemical, radiological, or nuclear weapons beyond what an expert could find independently?
- **Cyberoffense**: Can the model autonomously develop and deploy novel cyberweapons?

### Mandatory Third-Party Evaluation

Starting with ASL-3 capable models, we now require independent third-party evaluation of our safety cases before deployment. This includes red-teaming by external security researchers and capability assessments by independent evaluators.

### Expanded Employee Commitments

We're strengthening our internal commitments to ensure that AXEROAI employees have clear pathways to raise safety concerns, including anonymous reporting mechanisms and a Safety Review Board with genuine authority to pause deployments.

## Why This Matters

The capabilities of frontier AI systems are advancing rapidly. Models that were once considered experimental are now deployed at scale, and the next generation will be substantially more capable. We believe the responsible path forward requires proactive safety commitments — not reactive responses after problems emerge.

The RSP is not a ceiling on what we'll build. It's a framework for building wisely.

## What Comes Next

We are currently evaluating our latest models against ASL-3 thresholds and expect to publish our evaluation results in the coming months. We also plan to engage with policymakers, peer institutions, and the broader AI safety community to refine these standards over time.

We welcome feedback on this policy from researchers, civil society organizations, and the public. You can submit comments through our website.
